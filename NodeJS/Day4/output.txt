The wind moved slowly through the narrow streets of the old town as evening settled in. The buildings stood close together, their stone walls worn smooth by decades of weather and passing footsteps. Lanterns had begun to glow in the windows, casting warm golden light onto the quiet roads below. For most people, the day was ending. Shops were closing, doors were being locked, and families were gathering inside their homes for dinner.
But for Arjun, the evening was just beginning.
Arjun had always been curious about the town. While others saw ordinary streets and ordinary houses, he saw mysteries waiting to be uncovered. Ever since he was a child, he had been fascinated by the small details people usually ignored — the old clock tower that sometimes rang at the wrong hour, the abandoned library at the edge of the market, and the strange stories older residents would whisper about places that no one visited anymore.
That night, Arjun carried a small notebook and a flashlight in his backpack. He had heard something interesting earlier in the afternoon while sitting near a tea stall. Two elderly men had been talking quietly about the “hidden door” beneath the old clock tower. According to them, many years ago the tower had been part of a much larger structure — one that contained underground passages stretching beneath the town.
Most people would have dismissed the story as a piece of local folklore.
Arjun did not.
As he approached the tower, the air grew cooler. The structure itself was tall and slightly crooked, leaning just enough to make it look older than the rest of the buildings nearby. The large clock face showed 8:17, though Arjun knew it had been broken for years.
He walked around the base slowly, shining his flashlight across the stone surface. At first, nothing seemed unusual. Just rough stone, patches of moss, and cracks where time had slowly worn away the edges.
Then he noticed something.
One stone block near the ground looked slightly different. Its edges were smoother, and unlike the surrounding stones, it had a faint circular mark carved into the center.
Arjun crouched down and brushed away some dust.
The mark looked like a symbol — perhaps a handle that had once been used to open something.
His heart began to beat faster.
He pressed gently against the stone. Nothing happened.
He tried again, this time pushing harder.
Still nothing.
or a moment he wondered if the story had been wrong. Perhaps the old men had simply been remembering a legend that had changed over time.
But then he noticed something else.
The stone didn’t move inward.
It rotated.
Very slowly, the block turned under the pressure of his hand. A quiet grinding sound echoed through the base of the tower, as if a mechanism hidden behind the wall had not been used for many years.
Suddenly a narrow section of the wall slid sideways, revealing a dark opening just large enough for a person to enter.
A rush of cold air came from inside.
Arjun froze.
The passage beyond the doorway disappeared into darkness. He could not see how far it went, but the faint smell of damp earth suggested it stretched deep underground.
or a few seconds he considered turning back.
After all, he had only come to investigate a rumor.
But curiosity had always been stronger than fear.
He stepped forward and shined his flashlight into the tunnel.
Stone steps led downward, worn smooth by footsteps that must have passed through many years ago. The walls were lined with old bricks, some of them cracked, others covered in thick layers of dust.
Who had built this place?
And why had it been hidden?
Arjun took a deep breath and began walking down the steps.
Each footstep echoed softly behind him as he descended further underground. The sounds of the town above slowly faded until the tunnel was completely silent.
Somewhere far ahead, a faint metallic sound echoed through the darkness.
Arjun stopped walking.
Someone — or something — might already be down there.
He tightened his grip on the flashlight and looked into the endless corridor ahead, realizing that whatever secret the old clock tower had been hiding for decades was finally about to be discovered.
And for the first time that night, he wondered whether finding the truth had been a good idea at all.
